About Me

I am a Hydrologist and Research Scientist at the Center for Hydrologic Innovations at Arizona State University, and the Co-founder of Tributary. My research operates at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence, Remote Sensing, and Hydrology, with a specific focus on forest and snow systems in the Western United States.

I specialize in developing Deep Learning architectures to process CubeSat imagery, enabling the detection of forest thinning, intermittent snow cover, and streamflow dynamics. My work aims to bridge the gap between academic innovation and real-world water management solutions.
